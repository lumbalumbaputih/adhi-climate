===========================================================

--- One-line (LinkedIn headline / CV project title) ---
Physical Climate Risk: Tropical Cyclone Trends Affecting Western Australia
(1985 to 2024): a reproducible data analysis written for AASB S2 physical-risk
assessment (Australia's mandatory climate-disclosure standard).
[github.com/<user>/adhi-climate]


--- Three-sentence CV blurb (drop-in) ---
I analysed 40 years of IBTrACS best-track data (the official storm-path records
that weather agencies keep) and NOAA ERSSTv5 sea-surface-temperature data in
Python (758 South Indian Ocean systems, 194 affecting Western Australia) to test
whether the strength of WA-affecting tropical cyclones changed between 1985 and
2024, and whether it rises and falls with ocean warming, framed as an AASB S2
physical-risk assessment. I found that although the seas off WA warmed about
0.5 degrees C over the period, the storm-intensity record cannot settle the
direction of change: the Australian and US wind records disagree in sign for the
same storms, neither WA trend is significant, and detrended sea-surface
temperature shows no year-to-year link with storm strength, which means
physical-risk assessment has to rely on forward-looking climate projections
rather than simply extending the recent observed record in either direction. I
delivered a fully reproducible analysis, with the statistical routines
(Mann-Kendall with trend-free prewhitening, Sen's slope, OLS regression) written
from scratch and validated against textbook values, plus seven
publication-quality charts, published on GitHub.


--- Interview headline (say this in about 15 seconds) ---
"I asked whether tropical cyclones hitting WA have gotten stronger as the ocean
warmed. Over 1985 to 2024 the regional sea surface warmed about half a degree, but
the storm record could not settle the direction: the Australian wind record drifts
down, the US record drifts up, for the same storms, and neither is significant.
There was also no year-to-year link to sea-surface temperature. The honest takeaway
for climate risk is that you cannot project the local record forward in either
direction; you need forward-looking projections, which is exactly the judgement
AASB S2 physical-risk disclosure asks companies to make."
