Scored from primary documents: Woodside Annual Report 2025 (Sustainability Report, sections 3.6.2 Governance, 3.6.3 Risk management, 3.6.7-3.6.8 Climate, and the Remuneration Report) and the 2025 Climate and Sustainability Summary. Full text extracted locally with pdftotext; citations by the report's own section and page references.

## Reporting status (important context)

Woodside is a **December year-end (calendar year) reporter and a Group 1 entity, so its CY2025 report is its FIRST MANDATORY AASB S2 disclosure.** The report states explicitly that "as a calendar year reporter, Woodside is required under the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) and AASB S2 to include specific climate-related disclosures in this Sustainability Report." Unlike BHP (whose FY2025 report is still voluntary/TCFD), Woodside is already reporting under the mandatory regime. That shows in the structure and completeness of the disclosure.

## The key insight: high disclosure readiness is not low climate risk

Woodside scores higher than BHP on disclosure readiness (3.4 vs 3.0), because as a first-mandatory reporter its disclosure is already built to the AASB S2 structure. But disclosure quality and climate risk are different things. The same report shows an LNG-growth strategy (Scarborough, Louisiana LNG) whose transition risk is precisely what AASB S2 exists to surface. An ESG analyst's job is to make that distinction: Woodside discloses well and is still heavily exposed. That contrast is the most useful talking point this company offers.

## Strengths (scored 4)

- **Governance (G1, G3-G6).** Board plus Sustainability and Audit & Risk Committees; clear management accountabilities; strategic risks reviewed at least twice a year.
- **Internal carbon price (M8).** Explicit US$80/t CO2-e (real 2024) applied in capital allocation and abatement decisions, with methane effectively priced at ~US$6,720/t. Most disclosers do not state a price.
- **Remuneration link (M9, G5).** Climate is an explicit 15% of the executive Company Scorecard (gross Scope 1+2 plus new-energy progress), and offsets are excluded from that measure. Quantified and credible.
- **Performance + assurance (M13).** 2025 net target met; gross Scope 1+2 (6,616 kt) externally assured.
- **Risk integration (R6)** and **risk identification (R1).**

## Key gaps and credibility flags

- **Offset reliance (M12).** The net-equity Scope 1+2 target is met partly with carbon credits (1,283 kt retired to reach the 5,334 kt net figure against 6,616 kt gross). Woodside is transparent and prioritises abatement first, but the net target leans on offsets.
- **No absolute Scope 3 target (M12).** Only a Scope 3 *investment* target (US$5bn by 2030) and an *abatement* target (5 Mtpa) and intensity framing. For an LNG producer, where Scope 3 dominates, the absence of an absolute Scope 3 reduction target is the headline gap.
- **%-of-assets-at-risk metrics (M4, M5, M6).** Transition and physical risk discussed qualitatively; not quantified as amount/% of assets.
- **Net zero 2050 is an "aspiration"** (the report's own word), not a committed plan.

## Items flagged for your review / verification
