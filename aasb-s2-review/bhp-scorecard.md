# BHP - AASB S2 Readiness Scorecard (FY2025)

Scored from primary documents: BHP Annual Report 2025 (OFR 9.8 Climate change, Risk, Corporate Governance and Remuneration sections), Climate Transition Action Plan 2024 (CTAP 2024), GHG Emissions Calculation Methodology 2025, and the ESG Standards and Databook 2025. Full text extracted locally with pdftotext; citations are by the reports' own section and page references.

## Reporting status (important context)

BHP's Annual Report 2025 states its first **mandatory** AASB S2 report will be for **FY2026**. So this FY2025 disclosure is voluntary and TCFD-aligned (BHP maps to the 11 TCFD recommended disclosures, not yet to the AASB S2 format). The scores below measure readiness against the AASB S2 bar that starts biting next year for BHP.

BHP sits at the mature end of voluntary disclosure: a credible, Board-approved transition plan and unusually complete Scope 3 reporting, but with the quantification gaps that AASB S2 will expose.

## Strengths (scored 4)

- **Transition plan (S4).** CTAP 2024 is a full, Board-approved transition plan; OFR 9.8 honestly reports FY2025 progress and revises the pathway for technology delays.
- **Scope 3 disclosure (M3).** Full Scope 3 inventory (~378 MtCO2-e FY2025) broken down by category, with 84% from iron ore and steelmaking coal processing openly stated. Most of the cohort is weak here; BHP is strong.
- **Scope 1 disclosure (M1)** and **named governance oversight (G1).**
- A genuine strength inside M12: the operational target is met through **structural abatement, not offsets** (explicitly), and is aligned to a modelled 1.5C scenario.

## Key gaps (scored 2, the AASB S2 exposure)

- **Financial-effect quantification (S5).** Decarbonisation spend is quantified, but broader financial effects on position, performance and cash flows are limited. This is the single most common gap across the whole project.
- **% of assets vulnerable to transition and physical risk (M4, M5)** and **% aligned with opportunities (M6).** Discussed qualitatively; not quantified in the asset-percentage form AASB S2 cross-industry metrics expect.
- **Internal carbon price (M8).** Use of internal carbon price assumptions is disclosed since the 2010s, but the price per tonne is not clearly stated.
- **Remuneration link (M9).** A decarbonisation milestone sits in the social value scorecard, but the explicit percentage of executive incentive tied to climate was not confirmed.
- **No hard Scope 3 target (inside M12).** Net zero Scope 3 by CY2050 is framed as an uncertain "goal", not a target with a baseline and interim steps.

## Items flagged for your review / verification

These scores rest on cross-referenced sections rather than line-by-line reading, or on figures I could not fully confirm. Worth your eye before locking:

- G2 (board climate skills/competencies) and G5/G6 (remuneration link, management role) - sit in the Corporate Governance Statement (p.87-100) and Remuneration Report (p.104-112), which I cross-referenced but did not read in full.
- S6 - confirm whether BHP models a high-warming (>2.5C) physical scenario, which AASB S2's two-scenario rule requires alongside the 1.5C scenario.
- M2 - confirm both location-based and market-based Scope 2 in the Databook.
- M8 - confirm whether a specific internal carbon price per tonne is disclosed anywhere.

## Note on method

Scores are deliberately conservative: where I was unsure between two scores I took the lower and said why in the matrix `gap_note`. Confidence is tagged per row in `scoring-matrix.csv`. This is disclosed readiness, not actual readiness.
