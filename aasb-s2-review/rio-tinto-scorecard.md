# Rio Tinto - AASB S2 Readiness Scorecard (CY2025)

Scored from primary documents: Rio Tinto Annual Report 2025 ("Our approach to sustainability | Climate" section, governance and remuneration sections), the 2025 Climate Action Plan, and the Scope 1, 2 and 3 Emissions Calculation and Climate Methodology. Full text extracted locally with pdftotext; citations by the report's own section and page references.

## Reporting status

Rio Tinto is a **December year-end Group 1 reporter, so CY2025 is its first mandatory AASB S2 disclosure.** The report states it is aligned to AASB S2, the TCFD recommendations and all 8 Climate-related Financial Disclosure (CFD) requirements.

Rio Tinto is the strongest discloser of the three. It is the only one that actually answers the AASB S2 cross-industry metrics in quantified form rather than leaving them qualitative.

## Why it scores highest

The cross-industry metrics are where AASB S2 separates serious disclosers from the rest, and Rio addresses all of them with figures and page references:

- **Transition-risk exposure quantified (M4):** 82% of Scope 1 emissions covered by an emissions-limiting regulation; % and amount of hard-to-abate emissions; % of operating costs exposed to fossil fuels.
- **Physical-risk exposure quantified (M5):** % and amount of assets exposed to unmitigated physical risk, plus an annualised damage risk score, from bottom-up asset-level modelling. Best-in-cohort.
- **Internal carbon price (M8):** a disclosed price range and scenario parameters.
- **Executive remuneration linked to climate (M9):** climate measures in both the STIP and LTIP, with the percentage disclosed.
- **Both Scope 2 bases (M2):** market-based 7.5 Mt and location-based 8.5 Mt; full Scope 3 (575.7 Mt) by category.
- **Most ambitious operational target (M11):** -50% by 2030 (2018 baseline), versus BHP's -30% by FY2030.
- **Scenario analysis (S6):** multiple temperature pathways to 2100 including a higher-warming SSP2-4.5 scenario, satisfying the two-scenario rule.

## Remaining gaps

- **Scope 3 targets are largely action/intensity-based (M12),** not an absolute total-Scope-3 reduction. Given Scope 3 (575.7 Mt) dwarfs operational emissions (~31.5 Mt), this is the substantive gap even for the best discloser.
- **Full financial-effect quantification (S5)** is the strongest of the three (it puts a dollar figure on physical-risk-exposed assets) but still partial across position, performance and cash flows.
- **Climate skills matrix (G2)** and **SASB mapping (M10)** not fully confirmed in reviewed sections.
