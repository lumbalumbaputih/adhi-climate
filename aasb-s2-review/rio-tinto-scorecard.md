# Rio Tinto - AASB S2 Readiness Scorecard (CY2025)

Scored from the original documents: Rio Tinto Annual Report 2025 (the "Our approach to sustainability | Climate" section, plus the governance and remuneration sections), the 2025 Climate Action Plan, and the Scope 1, 2 and 3 Emissions Calculation and Climate Methodology. The full text was pulled out locally with pdftotext, and citations point to the report's own section and page references. (AASB S2 is Australia's new mandatory rules that require big companies to disclose climate-related information.)

## Reporting status

Rio Tinto closes its books in December and is a Group 1 reporter (one of the largest companies, which have to start reporting first). **So CY2025 is its first mandatory AASB S2 disclosure.** The report says it is aligned to AASB S2, the TCFD recommendations (the older, voluntary climate-reporting framework) and all 8 Climate-related Financial Disclosure (CFD) requirements.

## Pillar scores (0-4 scale, mean of sub-requirements)

(The four pillars are governance, strategy, risk management, and metrics and targets. Each pillar score is the average of the detailed sub-requirements under it.)

Rio Tinto is the strongest discloser of the three. It is the only one that actually answers the AASB S2 cross-industry metrics with real numbers rather than leaving them as words. (Cross-industry metrics are the standard, comparable figures every company has to report, no matter what sector it is in.)

## Why it scores highest

The cross-industry metrics are where AASB S2 separates serious disclosers from the rest, and Rio addresses all of them with figures and page references:

- **Transition-risk exposure put into numbers (M4):** 82% of Scope 1 emissions covered by an emissions-limiting regulation; the percentage and amount of hard-to-abate emissions; the percentage of operating costs exposed to fossil fuels. (Transition risk is the risk from the shift to a low-carbon economy. Scope 1 is the emissions Rio Tinto makes directly from its own operations.)
- **Physical-risk exposure put into numbers (M5):** the percentage and amount of assets exposed to unmitigated physical risk, plus an annualised damage risk score, from bottom-up asset-by-asset modelling. Best in the group. (Physical risk is the risk from the physical effects of climate change, such as floods and heat.)
- **Internal carbon price (M8):** a disclosed price range and scenario parameters. (An internal carbon price is a made-up cost per tonne of emissions that a company applies to its own decisions.)
- **Executive pay linked to climate (M9):** climate measures in both the STIP and LTIP (the short-term and long-term incentive plans), with the percentage disclosed.
- **Both Scope 2 measures (M2):** market-based 7.5 Mt and location-based 8.5 Mt; a full Scope 3 (575.7 Mt) by category. (Scope 2 is the emissions from the electricity Rio Tinto buys, and market-based and location-based are two accepted ways of measuring it. Scope 3 is the emissions from the rest of the value chain, including customers using its products, not from Rio Tinto's own operations.)
- **Most ambitious operational target (M11):** -50% by 2030 (2018 baseline), versus BHP's -30% by FY2030.
- **Scenario analysis (S6):** several temperature pathways out to 2100, including a higher-warming SSP2-4.5 scenario, which satisfies the two-scenario rule.

## Remaining gaps

- **Scope 3 targets are mostly action-based or intensity-based (M12),** not an absolute cut in total Scope 3. Given that Scope 3 (575.7 Mt) is far larger than operational emissions (~31.5 Mt), this is the real gap even for the best discloser. (Intensity-based means emissions per unit of output, rather than the total amount.)
- **Putting full numbers on financial effects (S5)** is the strongest of the three (it puts a dollar figure on assets exposed to physical risk), but it is still only partial across position, performance and cash flows.
- **Climate skills matrix (G2)** and **SASB mapping (M10)** were not fully confirmed in the sections reviewed. (SASB is one of the reporting frameworks a company can map its disclosures to.)
